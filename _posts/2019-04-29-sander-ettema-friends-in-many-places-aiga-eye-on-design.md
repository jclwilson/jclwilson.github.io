Working in an artistic capacity means oscillating between a state of dreary underemployment and frenetic bursts of overwork. Some would say this has always been the case, but these days it feels particularly acute. [Sander Ettema](https://www.sanderettema.com/) works almost ceaselessly, he's one of the most productive graphic designers I've come across. He seems to embody the energy of a saturated, sugar-hyped, Saturday-morning cartoon. But he knows this way of working is unsustainable, only a symptom of his anxious years spent trying to escape poverty. Writing for _AIGA Eye on Design_, I spoke to Sander about his first published zine, _Friends in Many Places_. Published by [Jumbo Press](https://jumbo-press.com/), which condenses the (un)justified fears and anxieties of precarious workers into glowing, gradated pages.

> I tend to get lost in this mania where I feel like there's always more to be experienced, I go over my own borders. I never have a clear line where the one thing stops and the other begins.

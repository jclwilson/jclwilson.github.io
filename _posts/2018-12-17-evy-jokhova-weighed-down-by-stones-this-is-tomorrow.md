I dropped by [Lily Brooke Gallery](https://www.lily-brooke.com/), London, to talk with sculptor and performance practitioner Eva Jokhova on her latest installation, <cite>Weighed down by stones</cite> for </cite>This is Tomorrow</cite>. Seeing the totemic pillar bristling with ultrasonic sensors, surrounded by mats and ceramic sherds, and hearing the staccato rhythms broadcast via bluetooth I wondered about how and where the line is drawn between archaeology and modernity.

> The modern perception of time is neither strictly circular and repetitive, nor linear and teleological, but oscillates somewhere between the two, cut through all parts by discontinuities.

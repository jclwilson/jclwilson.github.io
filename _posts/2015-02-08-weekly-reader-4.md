I've had a crappy week. The illness continues. I went to the doctor's and unexpectedly ended up spending the rest of the day in hospital having blood tests and chest x-rays. I'm still writing the essay. I have an extension on the grounds I've spent most of the week lying in bed wheezing. While playing about with my laptop I managed to wipe all the data from my browser. No more bookmarks, no apps, no history. About the only good thing that happened this week is that in the course of research I read a Daoist proverb on butchering oxen and dealing with difficulties.
- ["Whenever I come to a complicated place, I size up the difficulties, tell myself to watch out and be careful, keep my eyes on what I'm doing, work very slowly, and move the knife with the greatest subtlety."](http://www.bopsecrets.org/gateway/passages/chuang-tzu.htm)
- "The Daily Mirror headline read: '[Flick knives, dance music, and Edwardian suits.](http://www.vice.com/read/what-will-become-of-the-teddy-boys-282)' That, unsurprisingly, was enough to arouse conservative public opinion; before long, signs appeared in local dance halls reading: 'No Edwardian clothes or rubber soled footwear.'"
- "One of the best ways to do anything is to do it involuntarily. For instance, Yvonne, who is here seen volplaning through the ether, had no idea of going in for high jumping until her bicycle tactlessly wound itself about a telegraph pole.", The Courtauld's [Documenting Fashion](http://blog.courtauld.ac.uk/documentingfashion/) blog is back, and [on instagram](http://instagram.com/documentingfashion_courtauld).
- "After a one-year stay in West Africa in 2011, Malte Wandel came back to Europe with [a series of portraits of people that drew his interest during his journeys through Ghana, Burkina Faso and Togo.](http://aintbadmagazine.com/article/2015/01/malte-wandel/) Some he photographed spontaneously, while others he visited at home in their familiar surroundings."
- In the time we were at university together, Syed taught us more about looking and thinking about fashions and textiles more than anyone else ever has. [It's good to see that he's taken up the blog again.](http://www.dapperkid.co.uk/2015/02/the-jeans-experience-sensory-approach.html)
- "The change of pace is almost tangible: the textures, forms and subtle shadows come from a far more quiet and organic place than we're used to seeing in Viviane's work. '[I just naturally felt like I wanted to do black and white pictures again, and I hadn't done that in years.](http://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/viviane-sassen-2)'", Viviane Sassen: Pikin Slee is at the ICA London until 12 April
- "Whenever a woman is photographed, the issue of her vanity is inevitably raised, but Nixon has finessed this with his choice of natural light, casual manner and unfussy preparation. [The sisters never discuss what they are going to wear. Bebe Nixon says simply: 'We just wear what we feel like wearing that day.'"](http://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2014/10/03/magazine/01-brown-sisters-forty-years.html?ref=magazine)
- "Silicon Valley in 1990 and [there is no better way to accessorize a Polo Ralph Lauren shirt and Gap jeans than with a Mac Powerbook 100."](http://www.idea-books.com/appledesign.html)
- "Normcore knows the real feat is harnessing the potential for connection to spring up. [It's about adaptability, not exclusivity. Normcore understands the process of differentiation from a nonlinear perspective. It's addicted to the toolkit provided by YOUTH MODE and never wants to put it away.](http://hyperallergic.com/178944/blowing-up-normcore-at-the-la-art-book-fair/) Normcore doesn't want the freedom to become someone. Normcore wants the freedom to be with anyone."
- "It is a list compiled by nineteen year-olds thinking they might want to take photography seriously, and in that light I think it is remarkable. It tells you something about their 'world' and how photography might fit into it or express it. [It also seems uncannily like an inventory of all the major trends in 'art photography' from the 1960s to the 2000s.](http://davidcampany.com/what-to-photograph/) We didn't really know what we were doing but the results proved to be so fascinating that we put up the grid on our studio wall and it stayed there all year. I wish I'd kept the pictures. All 1296 of them."

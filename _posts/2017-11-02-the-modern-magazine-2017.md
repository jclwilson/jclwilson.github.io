Last week, I was asked by Jeremy Leslie of [magCulture](https://magculture.com/) to run [The Modern Magazine 2017](https://magculture.com/the-modern-magazine-17/) live blog for the <cite>magCulture Journal</cite>. I'd never before had a chance to visit the conference and I had a great time working alongside intern [Alice Daisy Pomfret](https://twitter.com/alicedaisyp), who took over the social media for the day.

The [early morning](https://magculture.com/early-morning/) session saw the day start with talks by Isabel Seiffert & Justinien Tribillon of <cite>[Migrant Journal](https://migrantjournal.com/)</cite>, Lydia Garnett, one half of <cite>[Accent](https://www.accent-magazine.com/)</cite>, and Tony Brook of <cite>[Unit Editions](https://www.uniteditions.com/)</cite>.

In the [Late morning](https://magculture.com/modmag17-late-morning-session/) session we heard from Anja Aronowsky Cronberg on fashion journal <cite>[Vestoj](http://vestoj.com/)</cite>, Owen Pritchard editor of <cite>[It’s Nice That](https://www.itsnicethat.com/)</cite>. One of the best talks of the day by Takahiro Kinoshita, editor-in-chief of Japanese culture magazine <cite>[Popeye](https://magazineworld.jp/popeye/)</cite>.

[In the afternoon](https://magculture.com/early-afternoon/) host Liv Siddall took to the stage to talk on the rise and fall of <cite>[Rough Trade](https://www.roughtrade.com/gb/rough-trade-magazine)</cite> magazine to huge applause. Mirko Borsche of <cite>[Bureau Mirko Borsche](http://bureauborsche.com/)</cite> fascinated everyone with his innovative take on print design. We also heard from a panel of publishers; Bertie & Char of <cite>[Mushpit](http://themushpit.co.uk/)</cite>, Danielle Pender of <cite>[Riposte](http://www.ripostemagazine.com/)</cite>, John L Walters of <cite>[Eye](http://www.eyemagazine.com/)</cite> magazine, Matt Phare creative director of <cite>[ShortList](https://www.shortlist.com/)</cite> and <cite>[Stylist](https://www.stylist.co.uk/)</cite>, and Paul Gorman, author of <cite>[The Story of The Face](http://www.paulgormanis.com/?p=19832)</cite>.

[The day ended](https://magculture.com/late-afternoon/) with Francesco Franchi, managing director of the Italian daily <cite>[La Repubblica](http://www.repubblica.it/)</cite> & its supplement <cite>[Robinson](http://www.repubblica.it/argomenti/Robinson)</cite>, James Hyman of <cite>[The Hyman Archive](http://www.hymanarchive.com/)</cite>, and Nicholas Blechman of <cite>[The New Yorker](https://www.newyorker.com/)</cite>.

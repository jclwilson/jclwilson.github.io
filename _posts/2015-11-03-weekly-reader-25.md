I’ve moved back and I’m properly working in Manchester now. I’ve been scoping out cafes to work in and testing coffees and pastries. I’m generally hanging out at <a href="https://twitter.com/northteapower">North Tea Power</a>, so if you fancy coming and grabbing a coffee or working with me, then just drop by.</p>
- Last month [Novara Media raised 10K in 30 days](https://www.opendemocracy.net/uk/james-butler-adam-ramsay/radical-media-novara10k-and-free-communism-interview-with-james-butler) to support their activities and pay their contributors. I signed up as a subscriber for £3 a month, well worth it.
- [A few weeks ago Hilla Becher died](http://www.nytimes.com/2015/10/15/arts/hilla-becher-photographer-who-chronicled-industrial-scenery-dies-at-81.html). I’d only come across her name a while back, but hadn’t looked at her work until a friend recommended it to me.
- <q>[They continued for a long time to look on us women artists as charming and gifted amateurs](https://news.artnet.com/people/words-wisdom-dada-genius-hannah-hc3ch-birthday-350867), denying us any real professional status. Thirty years ago it wasn't easy for a woman to impose herself as a modern artist in Germany</q>
- <q>[Jamky is a <q>postdigital</q> machine that creates drum patterns when small stones are placed onto its grid](http://www.booooooom.com/2015/10/12/invention-of-the-day-stone-drum-machine-by-devkid/). The project was developed by Ján Šicko and Roman Mackovič of Bratislava-based design studio, DevKid</q>
- [Jessica Dance’s woolly lamb’s cheese looks _almost_ edible](http://jessicadance.com/CHEESE-COUNTER).
- I’ve seen the film <cite>Alien</cite> and enough episodes of the <cite>X-Files</cite> to know that being alone in the middle of nowhere is a bad idea, but I would love to go and live in the []<cite>Halley VI</cite> base](http://hyperallergic.com/247625/antarctic-architecture-for-scientists-at-the-edge-of-the-world/).
- <q>My own fantasy of achieving full affective fidelity is a lost cause; I know I am far too fickle to ever feel the same way more than once. [Fidelity to ourselves, however appealing, joins high fidelity as an impossible dream](http://thenewinquiry.com/essays/the-lossless-self/)</q>
- <q>Hopping from village to village in a place that is as much sea as it is land, you do not expect to stumble upon a contemporary art gallery, especially one housed in a converted caviar factory and [exhibiting the work of one of Norway’s greatest living artists](http://www.port-magazine.com/architecture/kaviarfactory-norway-gallery/)</q>
- <q>Island (Oxford English Dictionary): <q>A piece of land surrounded by water</q>; <q>[A thing regarded as resembling an island, especially in being isolated, detached, or surrounded in some way](http://www.caughtbytheriver.net/2015/10/26/pomona-island-manchester-hayley-flynn/)</q></q>
- On Saturday I walked around Manchester with <cite>The Modernist Society</cite>, taking a tour of the inter-war architecture. On the website they've put up [a Northern propaganda film made by the Co-operative PLC](http://www.modernist-society.org/news-mcr/2015/10/31/things-we-didnt-see-on-our-inter-war-walk) to rebuff the notion that only London suffered during the Blitz. The film stands as a record of the pre-war city, and as an advertisement of the Co-op’s modernist architecture.
- <q>The Israeli government claims that they are obliged to take these measures to combat terrorism. The claim is a feint. The true aim of the stranglehold is to destroy the indigenous population’s sense of temporal and spatial continuity so that they either leave or become indentured servants. [And it’s here that the dead help the living to resist](http://www.versobooks.com/blogs/2298-john-berger-the-dead-help-the-living-to-resist-in-palestine)</q>

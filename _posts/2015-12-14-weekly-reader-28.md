A longer update as I haven’t posted in a while. I had a second interview down in London, but it wasn't meant to be. Instead, I got myself a tracksuit and I’ve started running every other morning - starting my new year’s resolutions early. I’m down in London next weekend for pre-xmas socialising, and perhaps a few exhibitions.</p>

 - With a triple entente of France, America, and Britain wrapping themselves in the red white and blue, it's pertinent to turn to Edward Luttwak's 1994 essay, <cite>[Why Fascism is the Wave of the Future](http://www.lrb.co.uk/v16/n07/edward-luttwak/why-fascism-is-the-wave-of-the-future)</cite>.
- <q>[We have been here before](http://www.lrb.co.uk/blog/2015/11/27/glen-newey/unwinnable-war-two/), with al-Qaida and then with the Taliban: Western governments have mistaken a super-decentralised network, somewhere between a franchise and an ethos, for an agency with a postal address.</q>
- <q>Excavated from <q>the realms of countries that are culturally Muslim</q>, the tracks that make it into one of Haq’s compilations are not likely to be found on world music shelves. Instead, [Discostan’s SoundCloud page is an anthology of field recordings, mobile phone mp3s and cassette rips](http://brownbook.me/discostan/) amassed from trips to the Middle East, North Africa and South Asia.</q>
- <q>For a few thousand people, the [Dancing Jesus music forum will wreak nostalgia](http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/the-story-of-kane-robinson-the-man-who-cost-the-music-industry-240million) for a time period when you deeply considered buying a Libertines jacket and were proud to know every word to Bloc Party’s <cite>Banquet</cite>. For those unaware, it was a no fuss music message board of around 12,000 users operational between 2006 and 2011.</q>
- <q>Germany might have Berlin and the States might have Miami, but the UK's got [London, Manchester, Leeds, Liverpool, Sheffield, and, if you're into really gabber and/or not paying very much for alcohol, Glasgow](http://www.vice.com/read/40-years-of-uk-club-culture-288)</q>
- <q>[By 1965 the first disco of its kind, Pipers, opened in Rome](http://www.anothermag.com/design-living/8074/the-radical-design-collective-that-changed-disco-forever). <q>Designed by Manilo Cavalli and Francesco and Giancarlo Capolei, it featured reconfigurable furnishings, audio-visual technologies and a stage for Italian and British acts from Patty Pravo to Pink Floyd</q></q>
- <q>Side roads lead to gated communities, sidewalks disappear into the driveways of residential cul-de-sacs, and outdoor malls, [the city’s most visible quasi-public space, perform civic and commercial functions unlike those of parks and libraries](http://hyperallergic.com/252203/a-fragmented-city-characterized-by-fences-doors-and-distance/).</q>
- [Jared Bark’s photobooth experiments are making me wish I’d take more photos](http://www.booooooom.com/2015/11/17/jared-barks-photobooth-works-from-the-1970s/).
- As is Mansun’s anti-selfie stick, [for people too embarrassed to use selfie sticks](http://www.booooooom.com/2015/12/01/hilarious-hack-of-the-day-the-anti-selfie-stick/
- [Magnetic ferrofluid clock by artist Damjan Stanković and Marko Pavlović](http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/liquid-magnets-power-a-killer-minimalist-clock).
- <q>Tap those keys on that keyboard gently—tap, tap, tap—not even pressing real hard, just let it come to you</q>. <cite>[The Joy of Writing about The Joy of Painting](http://lithub.com/the-joy-of-writing-about-the-joy-of-painting/)</cite>
- [The original clean eaters](https://www.npr.org/sections/thesalt/2015/12/03/457124796/death-by-coconut-a-story-of-food-obsession-gone-too-far)
- During my MA I did a project on authenticity and identity around food cultures, it’s fantastic to see the topic covered in <cite>The New Yorker</cite>, by Hua Hsu, <cite>[Chinese Food and the Joy of Inauthentic Cooking](http://www.newyorker.com/culture/cultural-comment/chinese-food-and-the-joy-of-inauthentic-cooking)</cite>.
- <q>But _farm-to-table_ is a (literal) misnomer in the sense that it only really focuses on how food gets from the farm to the kitchen. We need to push further. [We have yet to witness the emergence of a writing, or a politics, that looks at how food makes the rest of the trip, from the kitchen to the table](http://www.publicbooks.org/multigenre/on-writing-and-restaurant-labor)</q>.

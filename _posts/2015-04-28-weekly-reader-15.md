Another late post, thankfully(?) no-one's paying me to write these. I had good feedback on the dissertation, so I took time out. This week's list starts with a series of prescient issues that are being sidelined by the mainstream media's coverage of the general election.

- "It is feared that 1,600 [now closer to 2000] migrants have already died this year attempting to cross the Mediterranean sea [from North Africa to the European mainland](http://www.vice.com/en_uk/read/europe-or-die-all-episodes)."
- On last week's NovaraFM, Aaron Bastani was joined by Maham, Mohammed Ateek and Craig McVegas to [discuss the major causes behind asylum seekers taking the sea-route to Europe.](http://novaramedia.com/2015/04/journeys-into-europe-causes-and-consequences/)
- [The Photographers' Gallery interview Deutsche Boerse Prize nominee Zanele Muholi on her photographs of black LGBTI South Africans.](http://thephotographersgalleryblog.org.uk/2015/04/24/deutsche-borse-photography-prize-interview-with-zanele-muholi/)
- [How to Download a Genocide.](http://motherboard.vice.com/read/how-to-download-a-genocide) Hopefully the digital record of our own time will be useful in bringing people to justice.
- "P-DPA (the Post-Digital Publishing Archive) is an impressive resource created by Silvio Lorusso [dedicated to documenting projects at the forefront of modern publishing. It's far from comprehensive, but the user-generated archive offers up some exciting examples of progressive publishing.](http://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/pdpa)"
- "There was just a presence–a soul or spirit that's inside. It's so cinematic. I was there during the off season, but you could just tell that this was a story. And after they showed me the rooms that people sleep in and share for six months of the year, [we were like, yeah, we have to film this.](http://www.bonappetit.com/entertaining-style/pop-culture/article/birth-of-sake)"
- "[And I hope you have learned something during your journey. Now you must change to another bus for Reykjavik. I will return along the sea road to my home in Hofn, where the sun is shining.](http://www.mcsweeneys.net/articles/hofn-to-reykjavik-lets-take-the-bus-part-three-the-mariner)"
- "The pottery world is creaking under the weight of the amount of [thrill-seeking clay-spinners popping up all over the place making vessels for cool people to put their cacti and fennel seeds in](http://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/ceramicists-2015), and so we thought we'd highlight a few people who are taking the clay world by storm."
- "There's a way to create lush, intricate environments using drought-tolerant natives -- cacti mixed with different sages and ocotillos, and that sort of density and lushness that requires less water. [It doesn't need to be stark and barren. The desert is complex. The cactus is a complement to all of that.](http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/04/27/cactus-growing-tips-indoors/)"
- "[Of course, there is no telling what achievements and stats your frog will one day attain. We must look with hopes and dreams toward the future - but this story is yet to be told.](http://aliendovecote.com/uploads/twine/frog.html)"
